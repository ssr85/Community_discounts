# Community Spend Intelligence & Group Deals Platform
## Project Overview

### Problem Statement
Individuals lack negotiating power when purchasing everyday services (food delivery, groceries, home services). Spending data is siloed within Swiggy, Zomato, and other platforms — users have no visibility into collective spending patterns, and no mechanism to leverage community-level volume for better deals.

### Solution
A platform that aggregates voluntary spending data from community members (residential society, office building, neighborhood), generates collective spending insights, and uses AI agents to identify and negotiate group deals on behalf of the community.

### Core Value Proposition
> "You + your neighbors spent ₹85,000 on food delivery last month. We negotiated a 12% bulk discount — that's ₹10,200 back in your community's pocket."

---

## Key Scenarios

| Scenario | Without Platform | With Platform |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Food ordering | Each person pays full price | Community gets bulk rate from Swiggy/Zomato partners |
| Plumber booking | 5 people call separately | 5 people book together for 20% shared discount |
| Grocery delivery | No volume visibility | Platform shows ₹30k/month → negotiates weekly bulk delivery |
| Service discovery | Scattered reviews | Unified trust score from multiple platforms |

---

## User Types

### 1. Community Member (Consumer)
- Joins a community (society/office/area)
- Shares spending data voluntarily
- Gets access to group deals and savings insights

### 2. Community Admin
- Creates and manages a community
- Sends invites to neighbors/colleagues
- Monitors community-level spending reports

### 3. Service Provider (Restaurant / Shop / Contractor)
- Receives bulk deal proposals from communities
- Offers discounts in exchange for guaranteed volume
- Gets discovery and engagement via platform
